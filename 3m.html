01 / Executive Summary

Quality compounder, but priced ahead of the cash flows.

A NOPAT / invested-capital DCF values 3M at $135.67 per share against a market price of $150.78 — roughly a 10% premium. The valuation explicitly deducts 3M's two largest litigation settlements — the PFAS public-water-supplier and Combat Arms earplugs cases (~$17.4B of total scheduled payments, carried at a ~$10.0B remaining liability) — from equity value. Even with a constructive margin-recovery path baked in, the market is pricing in more than the modeled free cash flows support.

Take-away: The $150.78 price implies the market expects either (a) a faster litigation-overhang clearance than the settlement schedules imply, (b) margin recovery beyond the modeled ~1,000 bps of gross + SG&A improvement, or (c) a lower discount rate than 3M's leverage and beta justify. The base case supports a cautious / modestly overvalued stance pending one of those conditions changing.
02 / Company Snapshot

A leaner, three-segment 3M after the Solventum spin.

Following the April 2024 spinoff of its Health Care business as Solventum, 3M is a three-segment diversified industrial: Safety & Industrial, Transportation & Electronics, and Consumer. FY2025 continuing-operations revenue was $24.9B. The investment debate is dominated less by the operating segments than by the litigation overhang and the pace of margin normalization post-separation.

05 / Beta Calculation (CAPM)

60-month regression, cross-checked to market data.

Monthly returns for MMM and the S&P 500 over the trailing five years are converted to excess-of-Rf returns and regressed by ordinary least squares. The fitted slope (raw beta) is 1.072; a Bloomberg-style smoothing adjustment (0.67 × raw + 0.33 × 1.0) gives the 1.048 used in WACC. An independent Yahoo Finance estimate of 1.09 corroborates the figure, landing near the center of the estimate.

06 / Weighted Average Cost of Capital

Bond-level cost of debt, and the 8.06% blend.

Cost of equity from CAPM: 4.20% (risk-free) + 1.048 × 4.23% (Damodaran implied ERP) = 8.63%. Cost of debt is built bottom-up from market values — 15 USD tranches and 3 EUR tranches pulled from FINRA TRACE, weighted by market value and converted to USD — giving a 3.61% after-tax blended rate. With equity at 88.5% of capitalization on an $80.0B market cap, WACC lands at 8.06%.

07 / Litigation Modeling

The overhang, modeled as a present-value liability.

3M's two largest settlements are modeled explicitly as amortizing liabilities rather than ignored or lumped into a single number. The PFAS public-water-supplier (PWS) settlement carries a $10.3B present value over a 2024–2036 schedule; the Combat Arms earplugs settlement a ~$5.7B PV over 2024–2029. Each is discounted at its effective imputation rate per ASC 835-30, with imputed interest accruing on the declining balance. The remaining liability is deducted from equity value in the DCF bridge.

08 / DCF Valuation

Free cash flow to intrinsic value.

Free cash flow is NOPAT less the period change in invested capital plus non-cash operating items, discounted at the 8.06% WACC over an 11-year explicit horizon (2026E–2036E) with a Gordon-growth terminal value at g = 2.5%. Enterprise value is bridged to equity value by deducting net debt, minority interest, the remaining litigation liabilities, provisions, and employee options — and adding back the net deferred tax asset and long-term investments.

09 / Recommendation
Investment View · Base Case · As of May 2026

Trading ~10% above intrinsic value — cautious / modestly overvalued.

The NOPAT / invested-capital DCF anchors 3M at $135.67 against a $150.78 market price, with a 12-month target of $144.27 — still below today's price. The valuation already credits a constructive ~1,000 bps margin-recovery path and deducts the full ~$10.0B remaining litigation carrying balance. Closing the gap to the market price requires a discount rate below what 3M's beta and leverage justify, or a faster litigation clearance than the settlement schedules imply.

  • The overhang is quantified, not hand-waved. The PFAS + Combat Arms settlements (~$17.4B of scheduled payments) are modeled as amortizing liabilities; the ~$10.0B remaining carrying value is deducted from equity value — ~$19/share of the bridge.
  • Margin recovery is the swing factor. NOPAT margin expands from 14.4% toward ~24%. The stock screens overvalued even so — flatter margins only widen the gap.
  • The discount rate is corroborated. Raw beta 1.07, adjusted 1.05, and an independent Yahoo estimate of 1.09 all cluster — the 8.06% WACC is well supported.
  • The bull case is a re-rating, not the cash flows. Reaching $150+ needs a sub-7.5% WACC (β ≈ 0.8) or terminal growth above the modeled 2.5%.
  • Watch items. Faster-than-scheduled settlement resolution, a margin step-change from restructuring, or a decline in long rates would lift the base case.
  • Known model limitation. The beta sample straddles the Solventum spin; capital-return policy (buybacks) is not modeled — both documented and shown to not affect the per-share conclusion.
This view is anchored to May 2026 market data — MMM $150.78, Rf 4.20%, ERP 4.23%, β 1.048. Re-running the model on later prices, rates, or settlement updates can shift the call. The sensitivity grid in section 08 shows the range.
